About the Poet

My Journey with Words

For as long as I can remember, words have been my companions. From scribbled lines in childhood journals to carefully crafted verses today, poetry has been my way of making sense of the world.

My work explores the intersection of nature and human emotion, finding beauty in the ordinary and meaning in the fleeting moments we often overlook.

I draw inspiration from daily life, nature's cycles, urban landscapes, and the quiet spaces between conversations. Each poem is an attempt to capture something true, something real, something that might resonate with another soul.

When I'm not writing, you'll find me wandering through forests, exploring city streets with a notebook in hand, or curled up with a collection of poetry from one of my favorite authors.

My Writing Process

01

Observation & Inspiration

It begins with noticing - a detail in nature, an overheard conversation, a particular quality of light. I collect these moments like treasures.

02

First Draft

I let the words flow without judgment, capturing the raw emotion and imagery. This stage is about presence, not perfection.

03

Crafting & Refining

Days or weeks later, I return to shape the poem - finding the right rhythm, choosing precise words, and polishing each line.

04

Sharing & Reflection

When a poem feels complete, I share it - sometimes with close friends first, sometimes here. Then begins the next observation.
